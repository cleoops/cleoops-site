Here's the honest breakdown: what each major tool is actually good at, what you get for free, and one thing to try today — no affiliate links, no sponsored rankings.

## How to Read This

These tools overlap significantly. The question isn't which one is best — it's which one fits your specific use case. Read the "best for" line first and start with the tool that matches what you're trying to do.

---

## ChatGPT (OpenAI)

**Best for:** General writing, thinking through problems, drafting anything.

ChatGPT is the most versatile beginner tool. It's good at writing, rewriting, explaining, brainstorming, and working through structured problems. The interface is clean, the responses are consistent, and the free tier is genuinely useful.

**Free tier:** Unlimited access to GPT-4o mini. GPT-4o (the more capable model) is available on the free tier with daily usage limits — you'll hit the cap if you use it heavily, but for moderate use it's sufficient.

**Paid tier:** ChatGPT Plus at $20/month gives unlimited GPT-4o, image generation with DALL-E, and access to newer models as they release.

**Try today:** Open [chat.openai.com](https://chat.openai.com), create a free account, and paste this prompt:

> Act as a direct, experienced editor. Here's something I wrote: [paste a paragraph of anything — an email, a bio, a message]. Rewrite it to be 30% shorter and more direct. Then tell me the three most important changes you made.

## Claude (Anthropic)

**Best for:** Long documents, nuanced writing, anything where tone matters.

Claude's standout feature is its context window — it can read and analyse much longer documents than most tools. It's also notably good at writing that sounds like a human wrote it, rather than AI-generated filler.

If you need to paste a long contract, a research paper, or a full email thread and ask questions about it, Claude handles this better than ChatGPT on the free tier.

**Free tier:** Access to Claude Sonnet (a capable mid-tier model) with daily message limits. For typical use — a few substantive conversations a day — the free tier is enough.

**Paid tier:** Claude Pro at $20/month gives access to Claude Opus (the highest-capability model), higher usage limits, and priority access.

**Try today:** Go to [claude.ai](https://claude.ai), sign up for free, and paste this prompt along with any long document you have:

> Summarise this document in three bullet points. Then tell me the one thing I should know that's buried in the detail.

## Perplexity

**Best for:** Research, fact-checking, anything where sources matter.

Perplexity is an AI search engine. Unlike ChatGPT or Claude, it searches the web in real time and cites its sources inline. This makes it significantly better for research tasks — you can see exactly where the information comes from and click through to verify.

It's not a writing tool. Use it when you need answers you can trust, not when you need prose you can publish.

**Free tier:** Unlimited standard searches. The free tier uses a combination of their own model and third-party models. It's sufficient for most research tasks.

**Paid tier:** Perplexity Pro at $20/month adds access to GPT-4o, Claude, and their highest-tier model, plus unlimited file uploads and image generation.

**Try today:** Go to [perplexity.ai](https://perplexity.ai) and ask:

> What are the most significant AI tools released in the last six months, and what are they specifically good at?

Notice how the answer cites sources — click a few to verify. That's the behaviour to compare against ChatGPT's answer to the same question.

## Gemini (Google)

**Best for:** Google Workspace integration, multimodal tasks, anything involving your Gmail or Docs.

Gemini's main advantage is its integration with Google's ecosystem. If you're a heavy Google Workspace user — Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Drive — Gemini can work across those products in ways the others can't.

As a standalone AI assistant, it's capable but not significantly differentiated from ChatGPT for most tasks. The free tier is solid, and the Google One AI Premium plan adds Gemini to your Gmail sidebar.

**Free tier:** Generous — Gemini 1.5 Flash with effectively unlimited use for standard tasks. Gemini 1.5 Pro access with limits.

**Paid tier:** Google One AI Premium at $20/month adds Gemini Advanced (1.5 Pro) and integration across Gmail, Docs, and Drive.

**Try today:** Go to [gemini.google.com](https://gemini.google.com) and ask it something you've recently searched on Google — but ask it as a question requiring synthesis, not just a lookup. See how the response compares.

---

## DALL-E (OpenAI)

**Best for:** Generating images from text descriptions.

DALL-E is not a text AI — it generates images. You describe what you want, it creates it. It's built into ChatGPT Plus, so if you're already paying for that, you have access.

The images aren't always accurate on detail, but for quick visuals — social graphics, concept illustrations, rough mockups — it's fast and free enough to experiment.

**Free tier:** Limited generations per day via ChatGPT free tier. Enough to try it.

**Paid tier:** Unlimited via ChatGPT Plus ($20/month).

**Try today:** In ChatGPT (free or Plus), type:

> Create a simple, clean illustration of a person sitting at a desk looking at a glowing laptop screen. Minimalist style, warm colour palette, no text.

## The Honest Summary

| Tool | Best use | Free tier |
|---|---|---|
| ChatGPT | Writing, brainstorming, drafting | Good — limited daily GPT-4o |
| Claude | Long docs, nuanced tone | Good — daily message limit |
| Perplexity | Research with sources | Excellent — unlimited searches |
| Gemini | Google Workspace users | Excellent — generous limits |
| DALL-E | Image generation | Limited — a few per day |

**If you have to pick one to start:** ChatGPT. The interface is the most beginner-friendly, the free tier is useful, and it's the most versatile for writing tasks.

**If you need to research something:** Perplexity. Every time, for anything where sources matter.

**If you work in Google Docs all day:** Gemini.

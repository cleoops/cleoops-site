## Call Agendas and Talking Points

---

### PERPLEXITY CALL -- Dmitry Shevelenko or Jessica Chan
**Objective:** Establish whether there's appetite for a publisher bridge partnership

**Mins 0-5 -- Context setting**
- Introduce OpenX: scale, publisher relationships, programmatic infrastructure
- Ask: how is Perplexity currently thinking about its relationship with the open web publishing ecosystem?
- Let them talk first. Understand their internal temperature before you pitch anything.

**Mins 5-15 -- Share what you're seeing**
- Share the referral data: 348K YTD from perplexity.ai to ad-served pages on OpenX supply
- Frame as opportunity: high-intent traffic, currently unmonetised by anyone
- Ask: are publishers raising this with Perplexity directly? What are you hearing?
- Ask: does Perplexity have commercial ambitions for the open web beyond the product UI?

**Mins 15-25 -- The proposition**
- OpenX can classify and package AI-origin traffic across premium publisher supply
- Publishers get a commercial framework for Perplexity-referred users: better CPMs, direct deals, verified inventory
- For Perplexity: publishers become partners rather than adversaries
- Potential structure: co-branded publisher network -- Perplexity signals, OpenX infrastructure, publisher revenue share
- Ask directly: does this solve a problem you're actively trying to solve, or is it premature?

**Mins 25-30 -- Next steps**
- If interest: who else needs to be in the room? (product, legal, publisher team)
- If not yet: agree to stay in contact, ask if they'd share publisher feedback on an ongoing basis
- Offer to share the full referral data set as a starting point

**Key things to listen for:**
- Are they actively working on a publisher revenue share model?
- Do they have appetite for off-platform activation or data licensing?
- Who internally owns the monetisation roadmap?

---

### ANTHROPIC CALL -- Tom Turvey
**Objective:** Test whether off-platform signal activation is a conversation Anthropic will have at all

**Mins 0-5 -- Establish peer footing**
- Acknowledge his Google background directly -- you're talking to someone who knows this world
- Frame the call honestly: discovery, not a finished proposal
- Ask: how is Anthropic currently thinking about commercial partnerships in media and advertising?

**Mins 5-15 -- Share the signal**
- 135K referrals from claude.ai to ad-served publisher pages YTD
- Point is not volume -- it's what it represents: Claude users have high intent, they visit premium content, they're a valuable audience
- That signal is currently unrecognised and unmonetised
- Ask: is Anthropic tracking where users go after Claude conversations?

**Mins 15-25 -- The off-platform hypothesis**
- Privacy-safe activation of Claude-adjacent intent signals across premium open web supply, entirely off-platform, Anthropic controls data governance
- OpenX brings: publisher relationships, brand safety, independent measurement, no walled garden dependency
- Anthropic brings: the signal, the brand trust, the user relationship
- This is not an ad product inside Claude -- it keeps the Claude experience clean
- Ask directly: is this a conversation Anthropic would consider, or does policy rule it out categorically?

**Mins 25-30 -- Be honest about where you are**
- You're early -- testing appetite before building anything
- If interest: bring in OpenX data and product teams for a follow-up
- If not: ask what would need to be true for this to be worth having in 12 months

**Key things to listen for:**
- Does Anthropic have a policy position that rules out any commercial data use, even off-platform?
- Is there anyone else internally who owns monetisation -- CFO, CPO, BD hire?
- What's Tom's personal read on where Anthropic goes commercially in the next 2 years?

---

## What Both Conversations Have in Common

Listen more than you talk in the first half. The data gets you in the room -- let the question "what are you hearing from your side?" do the heavy lifting early.

The best outcome from either call is not a deal. It's a clear picture of who the real decision maker is and what problem they're trying to solve. Everything else follows from that.
